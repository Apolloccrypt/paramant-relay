post-quantum · eu sovereign · zero-knowledge

Encrypted file transport.
RAM-only. Burn on read.

Paramant is a post-quantum encrypted file relay operated from the Netherlands with infrastructure in Germany. No US owner, no CLOUD Act exposure. Files are encrypted client-side, held only in RAM, and destroyed on first read. ML-KEM-768 and ML-DSA-65 (NIST FIPS 203/204, Level 3) for authenticated transfers.

why this matters now

On March 25, 2026, Google accelerated its post-quantum migration deadline from 2035 to 2029. Six days later, Google published research showing elliptic curve cryptography, the mathematical foundation under TLS, SSH, and most digital signatures, can be broken with twenty times fewer qubits than previously estimated. This is a cryptography finding, not a cryptocurrency story, though coin wallets are part of the fallout. Adversaries are archiving encrypted traffic today for retroactive decryption. Data sent with classical encryption in 2026 enters that archive. Data sent with post-quantum encryption does not.

01

What Paramant does differently.

architecture
and ownership
cryptography

Post-quantum by default

ML-KEM-768 and ECDH P-256 hybrid key exchange. NIST Level 3 security category. ML-DSA-65 signed receipts for verified transfers.

storage

RAM-only, burn on read

Ciphertext exists in server memory only. Never written to disk. Destroyed on first successful download. HTTP 410 on any retry.

jurisdiction

EU sovereignty

Dutch entity, founder-owned. Hetzner Nuremberg infrastructure. No US parent, no US subsidiary, no CLOUD Act exposure.

proof

Public CT log

Every transfer hashed into a Merkle tree. Tamper-evident, externally auditable. SHA-256 leaf hashes, signed STH every thirty seconds.

architecture

Zero-knowledge

Encryption keys are client-side only. Relay receives ciphertext, cannot decrypt. Architecturally, not by policy.

deployment

Self-hostable

BUSL-1.1 source available. Deploy on Raspberry Pi, Docker, or any Linux VM. Free Community Edition for up to five users.
