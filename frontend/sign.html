ParaSign - post-quantum signature

Sign a document

Pick a document, place your signature where you want it, and sign locally. The document, the private key, and the signature never leave your browser unless you ask the relay to counter-sign.

Pick the document you want to sign

PDFs get a visual signature stamp you can place on any page. Other file types get a hash-only attestation that recipients verify by re-hashing the file.

Who is signing?

Your name is baked into the visible stamp (for PDFs) and into the .psign envelope.

Your name will appear as text inside the stamp. Choose 'Draw' if you want a handwritten signature.

Draw with mouse or finger. Use a steady stroke - the result is rendered as PNG and embedded in the PDF.

Upload a PNG or JPG of your signature. A transparent PNG works best.

Your signature style is what people see. The key is what cryptographically binds the signature - so it cannot be forged.

Advanced: counter-sign with the Paramant relay

Optional. With your relay API key, the relay also signs your envelope and writes it to the public CT log, so recipients can trace it. Without a key, your envelope is fully self-contained - still verifiable, just without a third-party witness.
