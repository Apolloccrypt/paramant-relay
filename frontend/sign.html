ParaSign - post-quantum signature

Sign a document

Pick a document, place your signature where you want it, and sign locally. The document, the private key, and the signature never leave your browser unless you ask the relay to counter-sign.

  1. Document
  2. Place
  3. Co-signers
  4. Identity
  5. Sign

What do you want to do?

Choose how this document gets signed. You can start over anytime.

Pick the document you want to sign

PDFs get a visual signature stamp you can place on any page. Other file types get a hash-only attestation that recipients verify by re-hashing the file.

Drop a file here, or click to choose

Stays in your browser. Nothing is uploaded yet.

Place your signature

Click anywhere on a page to drop the stamp. Click another spot to move it.

Click a page to drop the signature stamp.

Hash-only signature

This file type cannot be visually stamped. You will sign the SHA3-256 of the bytes; recipients verify by re-hashing the same file.

Add co-signers (optional)

Adding someone here makes this a multi-party signature. After you sign, you'll get a personal co-sign link for each person to send them yourself (along with the signed file). The email is a label for your reference only. Leave empty to sign just for yourself.

Who is signing?

Your name is baked into the visible stamp (for PDFs) and into the .psign envelope.

Your name will appear as text inside the stamp. Choose 'Draw' if you want a handwritten signature.

Draw with mouse or finger. Use a steady stroke - the result is rendered as PNG and embedded in the PDF.

Upload a PNG or JPG of your signature. A transparent PNG works best.

Checking your signing key...

Your signature style is what people see. What cryptographically binds the signature is your signing key (ML-DSA-65) — unlocked with Face ID / Touch ID / a security key the moment you sign, and never exported. Recipients you add sign at /co-sign with their own signing key.

Review and sign

This is what you are about to sign. Check the document, your signature, and the metadata. When you click Sign this document, you confirm with Face ID / Touch ID / your security key — that unlocks your signing key for this one document and the signature is created locally.

Document preview - visual seal

The stamp on the document is the visible Paramant seal. Drag it during placement; this is what readers see.

Your signature mark

Typed name, drawn line, or uploaded image - this is what sits inside the seal.

Cryptographic proof (.psign envelope)

The visual seal is supporting evidence. This envelope is the mathematical proof anyone can verify offline using only your public key. The signature itself is produced when you click Sign this document.

Share with your recipients

      
Each recipient signs at their own URL. Mail them the signed PDF + their personal link. Recipients verify the document hash locally before signing - their signature is added to the envelope on the relay.

      

      
Status of all parties is visible at any time via the envelope status page.

    


    

      
How to use your signature

      
Share both files with the recipient. They prove ownership only as a pair.

      
    
      
    Recipients verify in either of two ways:
    
      
      
        
    1. Open paramant.app/verify, drop both files in, and hit Verify. The page recomputes SHA3-256 of the document, runs ML-DSA-65 verify against the public key in the envelope, and (if the envelope was counter-signed) confirms the relay's signature against Paramant's published CT log.
      2. 
        
    2. Or run the CLI: paramant-sign verify <file> <envelope.psign> from paramant-sign. Same checks, offline.
      3. 
      
    
      
    A green 'verified' result means three things at once:
    
      
      
        
    • The document bytes have not changed since you signed.
      • 
        
    • The signature was produced by the holder of the private key matching the public key in the envelope.
