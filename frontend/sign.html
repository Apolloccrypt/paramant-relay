Sign a document

ML-DSA-65 (FIPS 204). Your private key and the document never leave this browser — only the document hash and the signature are sent to the notary. Recipients get a self-contained .psign envelope.

What is in a .psign?

The signature, your public key, the SHA3-256 of the document, a CT-log proof, and the relay's notary signature. The recipient verifies against the original document.
