ParaID · document tier (substantial)

Prove your age from your passport

Read your passport’s machine-readable zone with the camera. Your device checks the digits, works out only whether you are 18 or older and your nationality, and the registered issuer signs those two facts. Your name, birthdate and document number never leave the device.

First prove a live person is present. Run the liveness check, then come back here.

1. Point the camera at the passport

The camera is off until you start it. Nothing is recorded or uploaded.

2. The two MRZ lines

The machine-readable zone is the two long lines of letters, numbers and < at the bottom of the photo page. Read them off and enter them (44 characters each). Automatic reading is coming; for now you confirm them, and the check digits catch any mistake.

3. Get your substantial credential

Return to your wallet

  1. Why the check digits matter. Every MRZ field carries a weighted check digit and there is a composite over the whole line. One wrong character fails a check, so a garbled or edited MRZ is rejected. Integrity without a chip read.
  2. Honest scope. The camera cannot read the passport chip (that needs NFC and an app), so this is the eIDAS “substantial” tier, not “high”. It combines the live person (liveness) with a self-consistent document. A forged document with valid check digits is the residual risk, which the chip read would close.
  3. What is sealed. Only age_over_18 and nationality. The issuer derives them and signs them; it never receives or stores your name, birthdate or document number.
