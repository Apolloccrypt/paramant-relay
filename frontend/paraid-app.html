ParaID  Beta

Your ParaID wallet

Hold a credential on your device and prove claims like “18 or older?” with a yes or no. Or ask someone else to prove a claim to you. The data behind the answer never leaves the device that holds it.

1. Prove a live person is present

No credential without a real check. This one is genuine and runs in your browser: your screen flashes a random light sequence and the camera confirms a live face reflects it back. A photo or a replayed video cannot pass. Nothing uploads.

Not verified yet.
Run the camera liveness check

Honest scope: liveness proves a live human is present, not yet which human. This unlocks a presence-level credential. To also prove your age or nationality, do the document step: read your passport’s printed zone with the camera. We do not fabricate those attributes.

2. Get your credential

Once your identity is verified, the registered issuer signs a credential bound to a key on this device. The sealed facts come from the identity check, not from you.

2. Answer a request

1. Ask a question

Pick exactly what must be proven. Only claims the credential can actually answer are listed, so free text can never ask for something the passport never sealed. A fresh nonce makes every answer one-time.

Send this link, or let the holder scan the code. They answer on their own device.

2. Verify the answer

Paste the answer link you got back. It is checked against the public issuer registry and revocation list, offline for the crypto and one request for the registry.

Pricing. Free while in beta. Holders always prove for free. For verifiers, the plan is pay-per-verification (a few cents each) or a monthly bundle, billed to your Paramant account. No per-check fee touches the holder.

Beta: the holder key lives in this browser for now; passkey hardware binding and passport (NFC) issuance are the next steps. See how it works.
