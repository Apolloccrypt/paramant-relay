What is Harvest Now, Decrypt Later?

You don't need a quantum computer to steal encrypted data. You just need to collect it and wait. That is exactly what is happening.

Every day, intelligence agencies and sophisticated adversaries intercept and store vast amounts of encrypted internet traffic. Emails, file transfers, API calls, VPN tunnels — all of it. The encryption is currently unbreakable. That doesn't matter.

When cryptographically relevant quantum computers become operational — current best estimates put this at 2029–2031 — they will be able to break RSA-2048, RSA-4096, and ECDSA retroactively. Every piece of encrypted data ever collected will suddenly become readable.

If you sent a patient record over SFTP in 2024, it may be decrypted in 2029. If your legal firm shared a contract over email in 2023, an adversary may read it in 2030. The encryption strength you have today provides no protection against a future quantum attacker working on data collected now.

Which data is at risk

Any data with a long retention requirement is in the highest-risk category. State actors specifically target data that will still be valuable in 5–10 years.

Financial data

SWIFT messages, ISO 20022 transfers, audit trails, tax data. Financial data at rest or in transit under RSA TLS is being collected now.

Retention: indefinite

Authentication secrets

API keys, session tokens, private keys exchanged over RSA TLS. If the key exchange is broken retroactively, session secrets may be exposed.

Industrial / OT secrets

Firmware, PLC configs, SCADA data, proprietary process parameters. Critical infrastructure design data remains valuable for decades.

Short-term comms

Email, chat, short-lived API calls. Lower risk — the data loses value before decryption. Still advisable to migrate to PQC, but not the top priority.
